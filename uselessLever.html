Useless Lever

Go Back

This is a box that has a lever built out of LEGO on the outside. When the lever is pulled from one side to the other, the distance sensor is able to detect it. When the distance sensor detects the change, a servo motor flips the lever back into place. The robot also tracks how many times the lever has been flipped and how long it was being flipped for, and broadcasts those numbers through the speaker at the press of a button.


From this angle, you can see the button that is used to have the speaker say the time and number of flicks. Besides that, the button also stops the code from running.

From the front, the lever can be seen. You can also see the hole through which the distance sensor can see and the motor can push a LEGO piece through to wack the lever back into place.

From the top, you can see the LEGO pieces on the bottom of the box used to keep the robot facing the right direction, as well as a bird's eye view of the sensors and servo motor.
